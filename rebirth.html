reBIRTH: An eco-friendly app

Purpose:

This project was my design response to an open brief by CareerFoundry as part of the UI course. I am tasked to design the iOS and Android versions of a mobile app of my choice that solves a specific user problem. The inspiration for my app first came from insufficient recycling action(s) within suburban areas of Singapore. There is definitely awareness created, but more (actions) can be done to make sustainability a collective effort within the communities.

Objective:

Sorting trash may sound easy but do we really know exactly what the trash parts are made of and whether they are truly recyclable or should be identified as waste. We can help make communal trash collections easier by sorting our trash the right way. reBIRTH will serve as a handy guide to quickly help users to not just learn how to handle and sort their trash but also how to take action through the help of actionable information and features within the app.

Role:

UI, UX Design

Duration:

7 weeks

Tools Used:

Sketch, Invision, Photoshop, Powerpoint

My Process

ResearchThe first thing I did was to look at similar competitors (other apps promoting sustainability and green practices) and analyze their UX, functionalities, observing and noting down the differences between their iOS and Android versions.

View Competitor Analysis

Establishing the basicsI needed to organise my initial thoughts and ideas, hence I drew my user flows quickly into low-fidelity wireframes. As there are differences between iOS (Human Interface Guidelines) and Android (Material Design), I had to refer to them closely at the same time to ensure that I’m covering the grounds for both systems.

Mid-fidelity WireframeAs I progress to mid-fidelity, I noticed the missing steps when user navigates from point A to point B - my low-fidelity wireframes from before were insufficient to thoroughly convey the user flows. Therefore during this phase, I ironed out the flows more precisely so that navigations at any point makes sense.

User Testing with High-fidelity PrototypeHigh-fidelity wireframes were transferred to Invision prototype for user testing. The results from the interview brought up notable comments that helped me improve my designs further. For instance, the onboarding section seemed bland and caused some confusion, prioritising some information is required and the personality of the app wasn’t expressing consistently across as it should.

Conclusion

I’m very happy to find like-minded users expressing positive response to the concept of this app and see the need of such movement not just in Singapore but globally. Hence, I am very glad that the initial objective remained true to it’s nature till the end. I think analysizing the iOS and Android versions of the competitors at the beginning proved to be really helpful and really engaging as well. It helped me familiarise with the guides of the two systems. Though it was a little challenging initially as it was my first time diving deeper into the two systems and applying it directly to my designs.

Future Steps

The current image recognition technology will continue to evolve with greater precision. Ideally, understanding sustainability can be dived deeper as well. Rating parameters of how a type of trash is more sustainable than the other could be generated even more precisely to encourage more thought-provoking information on sustainability to the users. Potentially, involving more brand collaborations within the app would be beneficial to support reusuable items among the ‘trash’ items - it helps to prolong their usage. More actionable steps that complements with the nature of the app, for instance collaborating with relevant eco-friendly events would be favorable as well. At the end of the day, it should keep the movement going, to consistently reinforce the need of sustainability and it can start from simple habits.
